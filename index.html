Methodology: 2014 scores derived from 24 weekly Broadsheet Melbourne venue scrapes (Jul 2014 – Feb 2015) via the now-defunct Kimonolabs API; 10 pts per suburb appearance in the weekly top-9, normalised to 0–100. 2026 scores: curated weighted count of hipster-relevant OpenStreetMap venue types (cafes, bars, wine bars, vintage shops, art galleries, record stores, bookshops, cycle shops, craft breweries) per suburb, normalised to 0–100. Intermediate years (2016–2024): logistic-curve interpolation with suburb-specific gentrification trajectories and a COVID-19 dip factor for 2020 (Melbourne endured the world's longest cumulative lockdown).

Original project by mattwl (2015). Rebuilt 2026 with D3.js v7, Bootstrap 5, and OpenStreetMap data. Note: intermediate-year scores are modelled, not measured — treat them as indicative of the gentrification trend, not precise statistics.
