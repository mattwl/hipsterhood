Melbourne's inner-north has reshuffled considerably over 12 years. Collingwood has been the decade's biggest climber — jumping from fifth to second — as Smith Street and Wellington Street became the city's most concentrated strip of independent venues. Carlton North barely registered in 2014 and now sits fourth, a neighbourhood bar scene that grew quietly but persistently. West Melbourne and Brunswick East both entered the top ten from almost nothing. Fitzroy, the original creative heartland, slipped from second to fifth — not in decline but overtaken. Windsor and Pascoe Vale South, prominent fixtures in the original 2014 data, have all but vanished from Broadsheet's current editorial radar. Each year's 100 points are shared proportionally across suburbs, so these shifts represent real changes in where Broadsheet's attention sits, not just sampling noise.

The original version of this project was built in 2014–15 as one of my first experiments with D3.js — scraping Broadsheet's weekly venue rankings through the now-defunct Kimonolabs API and turning the raw numbers into a chart. This rebuild, ten years on, was generated almost entirely through conversation with Claude: the data pipeline, the chart, and this page were all written by AI. I described what I wanted, pointed out what wasn't working, and iterated from there. Same dataset, same D3 library underneath, completely different process. A reasonable marker of how much the tooling has changed in a decade.

Data: 2014 — 24 weekly Broadsheet Melbourne scrapes via Kimonolabs (Jul–Dec 2014), 40 suburbs. 2026 — hand-curated Broadsheet hotlist, April 2026, 100 venues. Each year's 100 pts distributed proportionally by suburb. Original project by mattwl (2015). Rebuilt 2026 with D3 v7 and Claude.
